Info and Care

Diet

Meat

Pretty much every fox hunts and eats small or sometimes relatively large rodents, so the proteins and benefits from meat is a very important part of their diet. In some cases owners will feed their pet foxes dog food as a substitute for actual meat. This can work as long as it is mixed with fruits, vegetables and bugs. It is important to note that if you do opt to feed your fox real meat it needs to be full animals and not processed grocery store bought meat. The foxes need all parts of the animal to stay healthy and happy. Likewise, most premium dog foods will give them these nutrients as well.

Insect, Fruits, and Vegetables

Like meat, insects make up a large part of a foxes diet in the wild. A breed like the Fennec Fox will actually find and eat much more of this than meat. So including real insects with even just dog food is important. Likewise, both fruits and vegetables can help your fox. Vegetables can be given with your foxes meals in small portions each day. Nothing more than a handful is about the max they need each day, and this can vary on the size of the fox. Fruits on the other hand, while not inherently bad for foxes should be only given as treats every so often. Foxes like many types of berries and cherries specifically have been shown to help with the more pungent urine smell some foxes have. Similar to dogs it is important to note that grapes and raisins have been found as harmful to foxes and should not be given to them. Finally, it is advised to not give your fox anything with grain in it as they do not eat this in the wild.

Enviroment

Foxes can not be trained in the same way as a cat or dog, and also require a different environment from them. It is not often suggested that you keep a pet fox indoors. The one exception to this rule in the fox world is the fennec fox that can be more easily kept indoors 24/7 than other breeds. That being said, all foxes need room to roam and play. The fennec foxes smaller size allows it to perhaps do this indoors as long as you have a big enough area, but nearly all other foxes need an enclosure outdoors. You can more easily keep a fox indoors when they are young but as they get older then tend to get more destructive. This doesn't mean you can never have them indoors but rather that you need to have an outdoor space they can stay in when you are not around or for any other reason. This is where the fox enclosure comes in. Fox enclosures need to have plenty of room for the fox to move and should be made dig proof and be enclosed on all sides. Aside from the basic structure of the enclosure there should also be many things to stimulate your fox while they are alone. This can include things for them to climb on and run through as well as a house similar to a dog house for them to sleep in. It also goes without saying that they should have access to food and water. Finally, foxes, like any animal, need to be entertained in order to stay happy so it is definitely suggested to give them fun toys and activities they can do with or without you. This can include regular dog toys, puzzle feeders, pools, sandboxes, etc. It is also good to walk your fox often. You should get them used to this at a young age and similar to a cat a harness is very helpful.
