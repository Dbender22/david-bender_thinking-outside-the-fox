Grey Fox

The Gray Fox is primarily a cream/orange color mixed with the gray that gives it its name. The top of its head and back are a gray and white mixture while the tail tip is a dark black. The underside of the coat is covered in various spots of red in a similar color to the red fox. They are typically about a foot tall and anywhere from 2 to 3 feet long. They are most commonly found in North America. Gray foxes are also known to be good at climbing trees.

Gray foxes have been shown to be very friendly and calm in the presence of humans, and for that reason make relatively good pets. They do not have a pungent smell that can be found with some other breeds but can still be an animal that needs a bit more training to keep them out of things they shouldn’t be in. Likewise, similar to most other foxes listed here they need a good area to play around in and shouldn’t be confined to a small space.
